## Full Description

### Stop Guessing. Start Estimating.

Every freelance developer has been there: a client asks "how much will this cost?" and you freeze. You pull a number out of thin air, pad it a little, and hope for the best. Sometimes you nail it. Sometimes you eat 40 hours of unpaid work.

**The Developer Project Estimation Spreadsheet** replaces guesswork with a structured estimation process. Break down features, calculate costs across multiple pricing models, build a timeline with risk buffers, and generate a client proposal -- all from one spreadsheet.

---

### What You Get

**6-Tab Excel Workbook (.xlsx)**

1. **Dashboard** -- Your project command center. See total hours, cost, timeline, risk count, and completion percentage at a glance. Every metric auto-updates as you fill in the other tabs. Includes a priority breakdown chart and cost scenario comparison (best case / expected / worst case).

2. **Feature Breakdown** -- List every feature with complexity (Low/Med/High), estimated hours, priority (Must/Should/Could/Won't Have), and status. Dropdown menus keep data clean. Conditional formatting highlights blocked features in red, completed ones in green. Totals update automatically.

3. **Timeline Builder** -- Pre-populated project phases (Discovery, Design, Development, Testing, Deployment, Buffer). Enter durations and dates auto-cascade. A built-in Gantt chart visualizes your timeline across 12 weeks. Buffer phase auto-calculates to 20% of total project time.

4. **Cost Calculator** -- Set your hourly rate and see three pricing models side-by-side: Hourly, Fixed Price, and Value-Based. Full cost breakdown with taxes, platform fees, and profit margin. "What-if" scenarios show best case (0.8x), expected (1.0x), and worst case (1.5x) estimates.

5. **Risk Register** -- Pre-loaded with 10 common software project risks (scope creep, key person dependency, client delays, budget overrun, and more). Risk scores auto-calculate from probability and impact. Color-coded matrix makes it easy to spot critical risks.

6. **Client Proposal Template** -- Print-ready proposal that pulls project name, scope, timeline, and pricing directly from your other tabs. Includes sections for overview, deliverables, and terms. Fill in the descriptions, export as PDF, send to client.

**Bonus:**
- Quick Start Guide -- Step-by-step setup instructions (15-minute setup)

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What format is the file?**
A: It's an .xlsx file compatible with Google Sheets, Microsoft Excel, and LibreOffice Calc.

**Q: Can I use this for multiple projects?**
A: Yes. Make a copy for each new project. The template is yours to duplicate as many times as you need.

**Q: Will formulas break if I add more features?**
A: No. Formulas reference rows 2-100 for features and 2-50 for risks. The Quick Start Guide explains how to extend ranges if needed.

**Q: Can I customize the proposal template?**
A: Absolutely. Change the text, add your logo, modify the terms. It's your spreadsheet.

**Q: Is the Gantt chart a real chart?**
A: It's a conditional-formatting-based Gantt visualization in the Timeline Builder tab. Cells are color-coded to show when each phase is active across a 12-week timeline. Simple, effective, no plugins needed.

**Q: What's included in the download?**
A: The .xlsx workbook (6 tabs) and a Quick Start Guide (.md).
