# Chapter 1: Choosing Your App

## Why the app matters more than the phone

The camera app you choose determines everything: video quality, alert reliability, battery drain, cloud storage, and your daily experience. A great app on an iPhone 7 will outperform a bad app on an iPhone 13.

We tested six popular camera apps and evaluated each on price, features, reliability, and battery impact. Here are our findings.

## Detailed app reviews

### Alfred Camera — Our top pick

**Price:** Free with optional premium at $3.99/month

Alfred Camera is the easiest way to turn your old iPhone into a security camera. Setup takes under 5 minutes: download the app on both phones, scan a QR code, and you're done.

**What we like:**
- Fastest setup of any app (QR code pairing)
- Free tier is genuinely useful (7 days cloud storage)
- Reliable motion detection with push notifications
- Two-way audio lets you speak through the camera
- Works smoothly on older iPhones (6s and up)
- Clean, intuitive interface

**What we don't:**
- No dedicated night mode (struggles in low light)
- Motion detection is basic (no person/vehicle distinction)
- Premium required for HD video and extended storage

**Best for:** People who want a quick, reliable, free setup. Great for indoor monitoring in well-lit areas.

### Presence — Best for smart alerts

**Price:** Free with optional premium at $4.99/month

Presence stands out with its AI-powered motion detection. Instead of alerting you every time a shadow moves, it can distinguish between people, vehicles, and general movement. This dramatically reduces false notifications.

**What we like:**
- AI motion detection (person/vehicle recognition)
- Night mode for low-light environments
- Two-way audio included
- Remote viewing with live streaming
- Good balance of features and battery usage

**What we don't:**
- Setup is slightly more involved than Alfred
- Free cloud storage is limited
- Premium price is higher ($4.99/mo)

**Best for:** Users who want fewer false alerts and need the camera to work in low-light conditions. Good for outdoor or mixed (indoor/outdoor) setups.

### AtHome Camera — Best for battery life

**Price:** Free with optional premium at $2.99/month

If your old iPhone won't have access to a power outlet, AtHome Camera is the clear winner. It has the lowest battery consumption of any app we tested, making it viable for battery-only use.

**What we like:**
- Lowest battery drain of any app tested
- Local-only storage (no cloud fees, better privacy)
- Simple, straightforward interface
- Cheapest premium tier ($2.99/mo)

**What we don't:**
- No night mode
- No two-way audio
- No cloud storage (local only)
- Remote viewing requires same Wi-Fi or premium

**Best for:** Battery-powered setups, privacy-conscious users, or anyone who wants the simplest possible camera with minimal cost.

### iVCam — Best video quality

**Price:** Free with optional premium at $4.99/month

iVCam started as a webcam app but has evolved into a capable security camera. It offers the highest video quality and customizable motion detection zones.

**What we like:**
- Best video quality of any free app
- Custom motion detection zones (monitor specific areas)
- Night mode with infrared support
- Also works as a webcam for video calls
- Two-way audio included

**What we don't:**
- Originally designed as webcam (security features feel secondary)
- Medium battery impact
- Windows integration better than Mac

**Best for:** Users who want the best possible video quality and fine-tuned motion detection. Also great if you need a webcam occasionally.

### Manything — Best cloud storage

**Price:** Free with optional premium at $3.99/month

Manything offers the most generous free cloud storage tier: 14 days of 720p video. If automatic cloud recording is your top priority, this is the app.

**What we like:**
- 14 days free cloud storage (720p)
- AI-powered motion detection
- Night mode included
- Two-way audio included
- IFTTT integration for automation

**What we don't:**
- Free tier limited to 720p resolution
- Medium battery impact
- App can be slow on older iPhones

**Best for:** Users who want automatic cloud recording without paying for a subscription. Good for documenting events and reviewing footage later.

### EpocCam — Webcam only (not recommended for security)

**Price:** Free with watermark / $7.99 one-time purchase

EpocCam is primarily a webcam replacement app, not a security camera. We include it for reference since some people search for it as a camera solution. It lacks motion detection, cloud storage, and night mode — making it unsuitable for security purposes.

**Best for:** Using your iPhone as a webcam for video calls. Not recommended for security camera use.

## Decision flowchart

1. **Is the phone plugged in?**
   - No → Use **AtHome Camera**
   - Yes → Continue

2. **Do you need night mode?**
   - Yes → Use **Presence** (or iVCam)
   - No → Continue

3. **Is cloud storage important?**
   - Yes → Use **Manything**
   - No → Use **Alfred Camera**

4. **Still not sure?**

## What about paid alternatives?

There are paid-only security camera apps available. We focused on apps with free tiers because the goal of this guide is to help you set up home monitoring without ongoing costs. The free tiers of Alfred, Presence, and AtHome are genuinely capable — most people won't need to upgrade to premium.

If you do want premium features, Alfred at $3.99/month or AtHome at $2.99/month are the most affordable options.
