# Introduction: Why Repurpose Your Old iPhone

## You already own a security camera

Right now, there's probably an old iPhone sitting in a drawer somewhere in your home. Maybe the screen is cracked, maybe it's too slow for daily use, or maybe you upgraded and forgot about it. Whatever the reason, that phone is doing nothing.

Meanwhile, you might be thinking about home security. You've looked at Ring, Nest, Arlo, or Wyze cameras. The hardware costs $50-300. Then there are the monthly subscriptions — $3 to $30 per month for cloud storage, smart alerts, and remote viewing. Over two years, a basic Ring setup can cost you over $300.

Here's the thing: your old iPhone has a better camera than most dedicated security cameras. It has Wi-Fi, a processor, storage, and a screen. All it needs is the right app and the right setup.

## What this guide will help you do

This guide walks you through the complete process of turning your old iPhone into a working security camera. By the end, you'll have:

- A working camera with motion detection and instant alerts
- Remote viewing so you can check in from anywhere
- Optimized battery and power management for always-on use
- Proper placement for maximum coverage
- Optional automation with IFTTT and Apple Shortcuts

## What you'll save

| Expense | Traditional Camera | iPhone Camera |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| Hardware | $50-300 | $0 (already own it) |
| Monthly subscription | $3-30/month | $0-4/month |
| 2-year total cost | $170-1,020 | $0-96 |

Even in the most expensive scenario (using a paid app tier), you're saving at least $100 over two years. Most people save $200 or more.

## What you need to get started

- An old iPhone (6s or newer recommended)
- A working camera on the phone
- A Wi-Fi connection
- A charging cable and power adapter
- About 15 minutes

That's it. No special hardware, no technical expertise, no monthly commitments.

## Important note

This guide helps you set up a useful home monitoring solution using third-party camera apps. It is not a replacement for a professionally monitored security system. The apps recommended in this guide are developed and maintained by third-party companies. Your experience depends on the app's reliability and your setup. For emergency situations, always contact local emergency services directly.

## How to use this guide

- **Chapter 1** helps you choose the right app for your situation
- **Chapters 2-3** walk you through preparing your phone and setting up the camera
