# Chapter 2: Preparing Your Phone

Before installing a camera app, you need to prepare your iPhone for its new role as a dedicated, always-on camera. These steps will maximize performance, minimize battery drain, and prevent interruptions.

## Step 1: Back up your data

If there's anything on the old iPhone you want to keep (photos, contacts, messages), back it up first.

**Using iCloud:**
1. Connect to Wi-Fi
2. Go to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > iCloud Backup
3. Tap "Back Up Now"
4. Wait for the backup to complete

**Using a computer:**
1. Connect the iPhone to your Mac or PC
2. Open Finder (Mac) or iTunes (Windows)
3. Select your device and click "Back Up Now"

## Step 2: Factory reset

A fresh start gives you maximum storage space and eliminates background processes that drain battery.

1. Go to **Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone**
2. Tap **Erase All Content and Settings**
3. Enter your passcode if prompted
4. Confirm the reset
5. Wait for the process to complete (5-10 minutes)

After the reset, you'll see the initial setup screen. Follow the prompts to set up the phone with:
- Your language and region
- A Wi-Fi connection
- **Skip** signing in with an Apple ID (optional — you can use a secondary Apple ID if needed for app downloads)
- **Skip** setting up Face ID / Touch ID
- Set a simple passcode (you'll rarely need it)

## Step 3: Update iOS

Update to the latest iOS version your iPhone supports. This ensures app compatibility and includes battery optimizations.

1. Go to **Settings > General > Software Update**
2. Download and install any available updates
3. Restart when prompted

**Minimum iOS versions for recommended apps:**
- Alfred Camera: iOS 13.0+
- Presence: iOS 14.0+
- AtHome Camera: iOS 12.0+
- iVCam: iOS 13.0+
- Manything: iOS 14.0+

## Step 4: Remove unnecessary apps

Delete every app you don't need. Each background app consumes memory and potentially battery.

1. Go to **Settings > General > iPhone Storage**
2. Delete all apps except:
   - Settings
   - Your chosen camera app (install in the next chapter)
   - App Store (for updates)

You can also offload unused apps: Settings > General > iPhone Storage > Enable "Offload Unused Apps."

## Step 5: Optimize settings

These settings changes will significantly improve your camera's reliability and battery life.

### Display settings
- **Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock**: Set to **Never** (the camera app needs the screen active, though most apps handle this internally)
- **Settings > Display & Brightness > Brightness**: Set to **minimum** (saves battery; you don't need to see the screen)
- **Settings > Display & Brightness > True Tone**: Turn **off**
- **Settings > Display & Brightness > Night Shift**: Turn **off**

### Notification settings
- **Settings > Notifications**: Turn off notifications for **all apps** except your camera app
- This prevents notification sounds, banners, and vibrations from interfering

### Do Not Disturb
- **Settings > Focus > Do Not Disturb**: Turn **on**
- Set it to stay on indefinitely
- Allow notifications only from your camera app

### Accessibility settings
- **Settings > Accessibility > Motion > Reduce Motion**: Turn **on** (saves processing power)
- **Settings > Accessibility > Display & Text Size > Reduce Transparency**: Turn **on**

### Privacy and background activity
- **Settings > General > Background App Refresh**: Turn **off** for everything except your camera app
- **Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services**: Turn **off** for everything except your camera app (if it requires location)
- **Settings > Privacy & Security > Analytics & Improvements**: Turn **off** all options

### Battery settings
- **Settings > Battery > Battery Percentage**: Turn **on** (helps you monitor)
- **Settings > Battery > Battery Health & Charging > Optimized Battery Charging**: Keep **on** if plugged in continuously

### Network settings
- **Settings > Wi-Fi**: Connect to your home network
- **Settings > Cellular**: Turn **off** cellular data (saves battery; the phone only needs Wi-Fi)
- If you want the camera to work during Wi-Fi outages, leave cellular on with a data plan

### Other settings
- **Settings > Sounds & Haptics**: Set all volumes to **minimum** or **off**
- **Settings > General > AirDrop**: Turn **off**
- **Settings > Bluetooth**: Turn **off** (unless your camera app uses Bluetooth)
- **Settings > Siri & Search**: Turn **off** "Listen for Hey Siri" and "Press Side Button for Siri"

## Step 6: Connect to Wi-Fi

Make sure the phone is connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your primary phone (the one you'll use to view the camera).

For the most reliable connection:
- Use the 2.4 GHz band (longer range, better through walls) rather than 5 GHz
- Place the camera phone within good range of your router
- Test the signal strength at your intended camera location before mounting

## Step 7: Disable auto-updates

You don't want the phone restarting for an update in the middle of the night.

- **Settings > General > Software Update > Automatic Updates**: Turn **off** both "Download iOS Updates" and "Install iOS Updates"
- **Settings > App Store > Automatic Downloads**: Turn **off** "App Updates"

Update manually during your weekly maintenance (covered in Chapter 5).

## Checklist before moving on

- [ ] Data backed up (if needed)
- [ ] Factory reset completed
- [ ] iOS updated to latest supported version
- [ ] Unnecessary apps removed
- [ ] Display brightness set to minimum
- [ ] Auto-Lock set to Never
- [ ] Notifications disabled (except camera app)
- [ ] Do Not Disturb enabled
- [ ] Background App Refresh disabled (except camera app)
- [ ] Cellular data turned off
- [ ] Bluetooth turned off
- [ ] Siri disabled
- [ ] Auto-updates disabled
- [ ] Connected to home Wi-Fi

Your phone is now optimized and ready for camera app setup. Move on to Chapter 3.
