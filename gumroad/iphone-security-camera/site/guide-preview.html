Chapter 1: Choosing Your App

Read the complete first chapter below. This is exactly what the full guide looks and reads like.

Choosing the right camera app is the single most important decision you'll make in this process. The app you pick determines your video quality, alert reliability, battery drain, and overall experience. We tested six of the most popular options and narrowed it down to three top recommendations.

What to look for in a camera app

Not all security camera apps are created equal. Here are the five factors that matter most when choosing one for an always-on setup:

  • Motion detection reliability — Does it catch real movement without flooding you with false alerts from shadows, pets, or lighting changes?
  • Battery efficiency — How much power does it consume? This is critical for always-on use.
  • Cloud vs. local storage — Do you want clips saved to the cloud automatically, or are you happy with local-only recording?
  • Remote access — Can you check the live feed from your primary phone when you're away from home?
  • Ease of setup — How quickly can you go from download to working camera?

Our top 3 recommendations

Alfred Camera is our top pick for most people. It has the easiest setup process (under 5 minutes with QR code pairing), a generous free tier with 7 days of cloud storage, and reliable motion alerts. It works smoothly even on older iPhones like the 6s and 7. The main limitation is no dedicated night mode, so it's best for well-lit areas or indoor use.

Presence is the best choice if you want smart alerts. Its AI-powered motion detection can distinguish between people, vehicles, and general movement, which dramatically reduces false notifications. It also includes night mode and two-way audio, making it versatile for both indoor and outdoor placement.

AtHome Camera wins on battery efficiency. If your old iPhone won't be plugged in, this is the app to use. It has the lowest power consumption of any app we tested, with local-only storage that keeps things simple and private. The trade-off is fewer features: no night mode, no two-way audio, and no cloud storage.

Quick decision guide

  • Want the easiest setup? Go with Alfred Camera.
  • Want smart, AI-powered alerts? Go with Presence.
  • Running on battery only? Go with AtHome Camera.
  • Need maximum cloud storage? Go with Manything.
  • Want the best video quality? Go with iVCam.

Preparing Your Phone

Step 1: Back up and factory reset

Before turning your old iPhone into a dedicated camera, you'll want to start fresh. A factory reset removes all personal data, frees up storage, and eliminates background processes that drain battery and slow things down.

Go to Settings, then General, then Transfer or Reset iPhone. Select Erase All Content and Settings. This will take a few minutes. Once complete, you'll see the initial setup screen.

Step 2: Update to the latest iOS

After the reset, update your iPhone to the latest version of iOS it supports. This ensures compatibility with camera apps and includes the latest security patches and battery optimizations.

Step 3: Optimize settings for always-on use

There are several settings changes that dramatically improve performance and battery life when using your iPhone as a dedicated camera. Disable auto-lock, turn off notifications for all apps except your camera app, enable Do Not Disturb, reduce motion effects, and lower screen brightness to minimum.
