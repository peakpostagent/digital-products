# Appendix B: Recommended Accessories

None of these accessories are required. Your old iPhone, a charging cable, and Wi-Fi are all you truly need. But these affordable additions can improve your setup significantly.

## Essential (if you don't already own one)

### Charging cable — $8-15
- Apple Lightning cable (iPhone 5s through iPhone 14)
- USB-C cable (iPhone 15 and newer)
- Get a **6-foot or 10-foot** cable for flexible placement
- Look for **MFi-certified** cables for reliability and safety
- Where to buy: Amazon, Best Buy, Apple Store

### Power adapter — $10-20
- 5W or 10W USB adapter (slow charging is fine and generates less heat)
- Avoid fast chargers for always-on use (they generate more heat)
- Where to buy: Amazon, Best Buy, Apple Store

## Recommended

### Phone mount / stand — $5-15

A mount keeps your phone stable and at the right angle.

**Tabletop stand ($5-10):**
- Simple adjustable stand for shelves and tables
- Adjustable angle for getting the perfect camera position
- Search: "adjustable phone stand" on Amazon

**Adhesive wall mount ($5-10):**
- Sticks to walls, cabinets, or door frames without drilling
- 3M adhesive strips hold the phone securely
- Search: "adhesive phone mount" on Amazon

**Clamp mount ($8-15):**
- Clips to shelves, railings, or door frames
- Flexible arm allows precise positioning
- Great for high placement (7-8 feet)
- Search: "phone clamp mount gooseneck" on Amazon

**Tripod ($8-15):**
- Small tabletop tripod with phone holder
- Most flexible for angle adjustments
- Easy to move and reposition
- Search: "mini tripod phone holder" on Amazon

### Surge protector — $10-20

Protects your phone and charger from power surges.

- Get one with at least 4 outlets and USB ports
- Look for UL-listed certification
- Search: "surge protector with USB" on Amazon

## Nice to have

### Wi-Fi extender — $15-30

If your camera location has weak Wi-Fi:

- Plug-in Wi-Fi extender boosts signal to distant rooms
- Place it halfway between your router and the camera
- Look for one that supports your router's Wi-Fi standard (Wi-Fi 5 or 6)
- Search: "Wi-Fi range extender" on Amazon

### Night light — $5-10

For night monitoring (since most iPhone camera apps lack true infrared):

- Small LED plug-in night light provides ambient illumination
- Warm-tone lights produce more natural-looking footage
- Place near the camera, angled to illuminate the monitored area
- Search: "LED plug-in night light" on Amazon

### Cable management clips — $5-8

Keep charging cables tidy along walls and baseboards:

- Adhesive cable clips (pack of 20-50)
- No drilling required
- Search: "adhesive cable clips" on Amazon

### Cable channel / cord cover — $8-15

For a cleaner look when running cables along walls:

- Self-adhesive cord covers match wall color
- Available in white, black, or paintable
- Search: "cord cover wall" on Amazon

### Portable power bank — $15-30

Backup power for outages or battery-only setups:

- 10,000 mAh provides 2-3 full charges for most iPhones
- Look for one with a Lightning or USB-C port matching your phone
- Keep it charged and near the camera
- Search: "10000 mAh power bank" on Amazon

### Waterproof phone case — $10-20

For outdoor or semi-outdoor placement:

- Universal waterproof phone pouch
- Make sure the camera lens area is clear
- Provides basic rain and splash protection
- Not suitable for submersion or extreme weather
- Search: "waterproof phone pouch" on Amazon

## Where to buy

All accessories listed above are widely available at:

- Amazon
- Best Buy
- Walmart
- Target
- Dollar stores (basic phone stands, cable clips)

**Tip:** Check dollar stores and discount retailers first for basic items like phone stands, cable clips, and night lights. You can often find functional options for $1-3 each.
