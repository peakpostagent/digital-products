# Appendix A: Device Compatibility

## Which iPhones work as security cameras?

Any iPhone with a working camera and Wi-Fi can technically be used, but newer models offer significantly better performance.

## Model-specific recommendations

### Best value: iPhone 7 / 8

These models hit the sweet spot of price, performance, and capability. They're widely available used for $50-100, support modern iOS versions, and have good 12 MP cameras. The iPhone 8 has a slightly better processor.

### Best budget: iPhone 6s / SE (1st gen)

Still supported by most camera apps. Good camera quality. Available used for $30-50. The main limitation is that iOS support may end soon, which could affect future app compatibility.

### Best overall: iPhone XR / 11

If you have one of these sitting unused, they're excellent security cameras. Powerful processors handle AI motion detection smoothly, and the iPhone 11's native night mode helps in low-light conditions (though this is separate from app-based night mode).

### Models to avoid: iPhone 5s, 6, 6 Plus

Limited to iOS 12, which means most modern camera apps won't install. The older processors struggle with continuous video processing. Battery life is typically poor on these older models. Unless it's all you have, consider upgrading to at least a used iPhone 6s ($30-50 on resale markets).

## Using an iPad

If you have an old iPad instead of an iPhone, most camera apps work on iPads too. The larger screen can be useful during setup, and older iPads often have good battery life. The main downside is that iPads are harder to mount due to their size and weight.

## Viewer device compatibility

Your **viewer** device (the phone you watch the camera on) can be:

- Any iPhone or iPad running iOS 13+
- Any Android phone running Android 5.0+
- Some apps also offer web viewing from a computer browser

The camera app on the viewer side is typically lightweight and works well on any modern smartphone.

## Checking your iPhone's iOS version

1. Open **Settings**
2. Tap **General**
3. Tap **About**
4. Look for **Software Version**

## Checking your iPhone model

1. Open **Settings**
2. Tap **General**
3. Tap **About**
4. Look for **Model Name**
