# Freelancer Tax Prep Checklist

---

> **Disclaimer:** This checklist is for informational purposes only and does not constitute tax, legal, or financial advice. Tax laws vary by location and change frequently. Always consult a qualified tax professional or CPA for advice specific to your situation.

---

## Pre-Tax Season Preparation (Do These Year-Round)

- [ ] Keep a dedicated business bank account separate from personal
- [ ] Save all receipts (digital or physical) for business expenses
- [ ] Track mileage for business-related driving
- [ ] Record home office square footage if you work from home
- [ ] Log all income as it arrives (use the Income & Expenses tab)
- [ ] Set aside estimated tax payments each month (25-30% of income is a common starting point)
- [ ] Keep a running list of major purchases and their dates

---

## Quarterly Estimated Tax Reminders

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes, the IRS requires quarterly estimated payments:

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Q1 | January 1 - March 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | April 1 - May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | June 1 - August 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | September 1 - December 31 | January 15 (next year) |

- [ ] Set calendar reminders 2 weeks before each due date
- [ ] Calculate estimated payment based on year-to-date income
- [ ] Make payment via IRS Direct Pay, EFTPS, or by mail
- [ ] Save confirmation/receipt of each payment

## Commonly Deductible Expense Categories for Freelancers

Use this as a guide when categorizing expenses in your spreadsheet:

### Office & Workspace
- [ ] Home office (dedicated space -- percentage of rent/mortgage, utilities)
- [ ] Coworking space membership
- [ ] Office furniture (desk, chair, shelving)
- [ ] Office supplies (paper, pens, printer ink)

### Technology & Software
- [ ] Computer, laptop, tablet purchases
- [ ] Software subscriptions (design tools, project management, accounting)
- [ ] Website hosting and domain registration
- [ ] Cloud storage and backup services
- [ ] Phone and internet (business-use percentage)

### Professional Development
- [ ] Online courses and training
- [ ] Books and publications related to your field
- [ ] Conference and workshop registration fees
- [ ] Professional certifications

### Marketing & Business Development
- [ ] Business cards and printed materials
- [ ] Website design and maintenance
- [ ] Online advertising (Google Ads, social media ads)
- [ ] Portfolio hosting
- [ ] Networking event costs

### Travel & Transportation
- [ ] Business travel (flights, hotels, meals)
- [ ] Mileage for business driving (IRS standard rate)
- [ ] Parking and tolls for business meetings
- [ ] Public transit for business purposes

### Professional Services
- [ ] Accounting and bookkeeping fees
- [ ] Legal fees related to your business
- [ ] Business insurance (liability, E&O)
- [ ] Business license and permit fees

### Health & Retirement
- [ ] Health insurance premiums (if self-employed)
- [ ] SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), or SIMPLE IRA contributions
- [ ] HSA contributions

## Records to Gather at Tax Time

### Income Documentation
- [ ] All 1099-NEC forms from clients who paid you $600+
- [ ] All 1099-K forms from payment platforms (PayPal, Stripe, etc.)
- [ ] Bank statements showing all business income
- [ ] Records of income not reported on 1099s (you still owe taxes on it)
- [ ] Records of any bartered services

### Expense Documentation
- [ ] Complete expense log from your spreadsheet (Income & Expenses tab)
- [ ] Receipts for all deductible expenses
- [ ] Bank and credit card statements for the business account
- [ ] Mileage log with dates, destinations, and business purpose
- [ ] Home office measurements and total home square footage
- [ ] Rent/mortgage and utility bills (for home office deduction)

### Tax Payment Records
- [ ] Quarterly estimated tax payment confirmations
- [ ] Prior year tax return (for reference and carryover items)
- [ ] State estimated tax payments if applicable

### Business Information
- [ ] EIN or SSN used for business
- [ ] Business name and address
- [ ] Business structure (sole proprietor, LLC, S-Corp)
- [ ] State business registrations

## Year-End Financial Review Checklist

Complete these tasks in December or early January:

### Income Review
- [ ] Verify all income is recorded in your spreadsheet
- [ ] Reconcile spreadsheet totals with bank deposits
- [ ] Follow up on any unpaid invoices from the year
- [ ] Note any income received in advance for work not yet performed

### Expense Review
- [ ] Verify all expenses are recorded and categorized
- [ ] Ensure all receipts are saved (scan paper receipts)
- [ ] Identify any missing receipts and try to obtain copies
- [ ] Review subscriptions -- cancel any you're not using
- [ ] Consider pre-paying deductible expenses before Dec 31

### Planning
- [ ] Calculate total annual income and expenses
- [ ] Compare actual income to Rate Calculator projections
- [ ] Estimate total tax liability for the year
- [ ] Evaluate if you need to make an additional Q4 estimated payment
- [ ] Consider retirement account contributions before deadline
- [ ] Review business structure -- is it still the most tax-efficient?

### Prepare for Next Year
- [ ] Update your hourly/project rates if needed
- [ ] Set new income goals based on this year's data
- [ ] Archive this year's spreadsheet (save a copy)
- [ ] Start a fresh spreadsheet for the new year (or clear old data)
- [ ] Schedule a meeting with your tax professional

## Key Tax Forms for Freelancers

| Form | What It's For |
|------|--------------|
| Schedule C | Report business income and expenses |
| Schedule SE | Calculate self-employment tax |
| Form 1040-ES | Make quarterly estimated tax payments |
| 1099-NEC | Income reported by clients who paid you $600+ |
| 1099-K | Income from payment platforms exceeding threshold |
| Form 8829 | Home office deduction (if using actual method) |
