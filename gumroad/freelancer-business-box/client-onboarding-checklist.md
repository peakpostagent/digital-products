# Client Onboarding Checklist

A step-by-step process for bringing new clients into your freelance business smoothly and professionally.

---

## Pre-Project Checklist

Complete all of these before starting any work:

### Agreements & Payment
- [ ] Proposal sent and approved by client
- [ ] Contract or service agreement signed by both parties
- [ ] Scope of work clearly defined in writing
- [ ] Payment terms agreed upon (deposit amount, milestones, net terms)
- [ ] Deposit or first payment received
- [ ] Invoice created and logged in Invoice Tracker tab

### Client Setup
- [ ] Client added to Client CRM tab in your spreadsheet
- [ ] Project created in Project Pipeline tab
- [ ] Client contact info verified (email, phone, preferred communication)
- [ ] Key stakeholders identified (who approves work? who gives feedback?)
- [ ] Decision-making process clarified (one approver or committee?)

---

## Kickoff Meeting Agenda Template

Use this agenda for your first meeting with a new client:

### 1. Introductions (5 min)
- Introduce yourself and your process
- Have the client introduce their team/stakeholders
- Confirm roles: who does what on both sides

### 2. Project Goals Review (10 min)
- Revisit the project objectives from the proposal
- Confirm the client's definition of success
- Identify any changes since the proposal was signed
- Clarify what is NOT included in scope

### 3. Timeline & Milestones (10 min)
- Walk through the project timeline
- Confirm key milestone dates
- Identify any hard deadlines (launches, events, seasons)
- Discuss what happens if the timeline slips

### 4. Communication Plan (5 min)
- Agree on communication channels (email, Slack, phone)
- Set check-in frequency (weekly? biweekly?)
- Establish response time expectations (both sides)
- Confirm who to contact for different types of questions

### 5. Deliverables & Feedback Process (10 min)
- Review what you'll deliver at each phase
- Agree on the feedback/revision process
- Clarify how many revision rounds are included
- Define what constitutes a "revision" vs. a "new request"

### 6. Next Steps (5 min)
- List action items for both sides
- Confirm the first deliverable date
- Schedule the next check-in

## Information to Collect from New Clients

### Business Basics
- [ ] Company name and legal entity name (if different)
- [ ] Business website and social media profiles
- [ ] Industry and target audience
- [ ] Brand guidelines (logos, colors, fonts, tone of voice)
- [ ] Existing assets (photos, copy, previous work)

### Project-Specific
- [ ] Detailed brief or requirements document
- [ ] Examples of work they like (and don't like)
- [ ] Competitors to be aware of
- [ ] Technical requirements or constraints
- [ ] Access credentials needed (CMS login, analytics, etc.)
- [ ] File sharing method preference (Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)

### Administrative
- [ ] Billing contact name and email
- [ ] Mailing address (for invoices if needed)
- [ ] Purchase order number (if required by their company)
- [ ] Preferred payment method
- [ ] Tax ID or W-9 (if they require one from you)

---

## Communication Preferences to Establish

Discuss and document these on day one:

- [ ] **Primary channel:** Email, Slack, Teams, or other?
- [ ] **Urgency protocol:** How should they reach you for urgent issues?
- [ ] **Response time:** What's reasonable on both sides? (e.g., 24 business hours)
- [ ] **Meeting cadence:** Weekly calls? Biweekly? As-needed?
- [ ] **Meeting tool:** Zoom, Google Meet, phone?
- [ ] **File sharing:** Google Drive, Dropbox, WeTransfer?
- [ ] **Feedback format:** Written comments, marked-up PDFs, live calls?
- [ ] **Status updates:** How often and in what format?
- [ ] **Out-of-office protocol:** How to handle vacations and time off

---

## Project Setup Steps

After the kickoff meeting, complete these:

- [ ] Create project folder in your file system
- [ ] Set up shared folder with client (for deliverables and feedback)
- [ ] Add project milestones to your calendar
- [ ] Set up time tracking for the project
- [ ] Create any necessary project management boards or task lists
- [ ] Send kickoff meeting recap email with action items
- [ ] Begin first phase of work

## Red Flags to Watch For

Be cautious if you notice any of these during onboarding:

### Scope Concerns
- Client can't clearly articulate what they want
- Requirements keep changing before work even starts
- "We'll figure it out as we go" without willingness to define scope
- Expectations that seem unrealistic for the budget or timeline

### Payment Concerns
- Resistance to signing a contract
- Pushback on paying a deposit before work begins
- History of late payments (ask for references if unsure)
- Wanting to pay only "when they're satisfied" with no clear criteria

### Communication Concerns
- Multiple people giving conflicting direction
- Very slow response times during the proposal phase
- Excessive urgency or pressure to skip your normal process
- Disrespect for your time or boundaries

### Process Concerns
- Client wants to bypass your normal workflow
- Insistence on tools or processes that don't work for you
- Unwillingness to commit to a feedback timeline
- "Last freelancer didn't work out" without explanation

**How to handle red flags:** Trust your instincts. Address concerns directly with the client. If issues can't be resolved, it's better to walk away before starting than to deal with a problematic project mid-stream. A polite "I don't think I'm the right fit for this project" protects your time and reputation.

---

*A smooth onboarding sets the tone for the entire project. Take the time to do it right and both you and your client will benefit.*
